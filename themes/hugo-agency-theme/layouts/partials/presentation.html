Improv for Businesses.

Some people think “Improv” means “stand-up comedy”. It doesn’t. Improv is a method that can be applied to music, science, and even your business.

Improv is a way of working spontaneously and collaboratively to deal with unexpected situations. Improv focuses on core principles like listening, embracing failure, reacting under any circumstances. This will help your team communicate and give your business space for innovation.

Those principles require practice but provide great rewards. Companies like Google, Apple or Twitter are already incorporating Improv into their training programs. The Harvard Business Review wrote “Improv Exercises can change the way your team works”, The Forbes Magazine describe how “Improv training is great for your business”.

Everyone can benefits from improv. Team, managers or individuals. Small businesses like start-up and large companies.
